# What you can do

- With Gemini-cli: You pipe the error directly from your terminal into the AI.

```bash
node app.js add | gemini-cli "Why is this throwing an ENOENT error?"
```

1. AI as a "Terminal Peer" (Not a Replacement)
2. Automating the "Chore" Commits
3. Rapid Prototyping for your "Roadmap" Challenges
   You’re building a Task-Tracker now, but what if the next challenge is a "Real-time Weather CLI" or a "URL Shortener"?
   You can use Gemini-cli to generate the boilerplate for the package.json or the initial db.js logic, and then you step in to do the heavy lifting. It saves you the 20 minutes of "setup" so you can get to the "problem solving."
4. Integration with your PDE
   As an Arch user, you love customization.
   You can alias Gemini-cli commands in your .bashrc or .zshrc:
   `alias explain="gemini-cli 'explain this code like a senior backend dev'"`
   Now, inside Neovim, you can visually select a complex recursive TypeScript type and send it to your alias.
