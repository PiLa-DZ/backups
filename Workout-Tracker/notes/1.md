Your service layer is complete.
Moving forward, the most logical and professional path is to build out the **Middleware** layer.
This will allow you to handle authentication,
global error handling,
and Zod input validation cleanly—essentially preparing the "pipes" through
which your controllers will communicate with your services.

Here are your options for the next phase,
ordered by standard industry practice:

### Option 1: Middleware & Global Error Handling (Recommended Next Step)

- **Why**: You have services, but no way to safely call them from HTTP routes yet.
- **Tasks**:
  1. **Error Handling Middleware**: Implement a middleware that catches any thrown `AppError` and returns a standardized JSON response.
  2. **Authentication Middleware**: Create an Express middleware that verifies JWTs (using your `AuthService`) and attaches the `userId` to the request object.
  3. **Validation Middleware**: Write a Zod-based middleware to validate request bodies/params against your `API-Schema.md` before they ever hit your controllers.

### Option 2: Controller & Route Implementation

- **Why**: This is the "face" of your backend.
- **Tasks**:
  1. Create the `src/controllers/` layer.
  2. Implement routes in `src/routes/` that connect specific paths (e.g., `POST /api/workouts`) to their controllers.
  3. Connect these to the `WorkoutService`, `ExerciseService`, etc., you already built.

### Option 3: Utility & Logging Polish (`src/lib/`)

- **Why**: You mentioned focusing on utilities.
- **Tasks**:
  1. **Logger**: Configure `winston` as planned in `wiki/Logging-Strategy.md`.
  2. **Utilities**: Create any shared helper functions (e.g., date formatting, slug generators).
